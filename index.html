Safe Swimming & Golden Sands for Families

A sheltered north coast beach with shallow waters, making it ideal for children and less confident swimmers.

About Ladies Bay

Ladies Bay offers everything families need for a stress-free beach day. The gently shelving sand creates shallow paddling areas where young children can splash safely, while the shelter from prevailing winds means fewer "it's too windy" complaints.

The beach takes its name from the nearby Ladies College, which historically maintained a beach hut here for students. Today, it welcomes everyone - though it remains particularly popular with local families who appreciate the calm conditions.

Sheltered Location

Positioned on Guernsey's north coast, Ladies Bay faces away from the prevailing south-westerly winds. This creates a microclimate that's often warmer and calmer than exposed west coast beaches. The water remains shallow for a good distance, allowing children to play safely.

Sandcastle Territory

The sand here is perfect for building - fine enough to hold shape but not so dry it collapses. Bring buckets and spades; competitions have been known to break out among competitive dads.

Visitor Tips

  • Best 2 hours either side of high tide
  • North coast gets more sunshine in afternoons
  • Limited shade - bring a beach umbrella
  • Ice cream van visits on summer weekends
  • Check tide tables - completely covered at high tide
